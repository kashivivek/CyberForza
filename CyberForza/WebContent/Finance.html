Finance

The financial services industry is faced with a growing number of ever-evolving cybersecurity challenges. Issues of utmost concern include stifling compliance regulations, the struggle to secure customer data and third-party risk.

With the introdution of new technology in the financial markets, hackers become more sophisticated in their approach and continue to breach financial institutions. To address this, Cyber Forza has commissioned penetration testing performed by 3rd party companies who were unsuccesful in penetrating the Cyber Forza suite of products. We understand the vulnerabilities that the financial market is faced with each day. By compromising on any of the below items will most likely have a significant impact on market share, customers and reputation.

  • Privacy and data protection
  • Compliance
  • Managing third party risk
  • Customer expectations
  • Emerging threats both internally and externally
  • Mobility

Use Case

It’s always challenging and critical to ensure effective security, without compromise for financial services with no network slowdowns given they have been deemed critical infrastructure.

A worldwide survey of over 900 financial executives conducted by Accenture Strategy found that over two-thirds of respondents believe the likelihood of a cyber attack to be “very” or “extremely” high (and a similar share sees a high likelihood of data or privacy breaches). But only 9% run inward-directed attacks and intentional failures to test their systems on a regular basis. There is a significant disconnect between cyber threat awareness and preparedness in most organizations.

CHALLENGES

  • Detect and mitigate web-based threats that have bypassed existing traditional legacy security measures & systems.
  • Accommodate financial institution diverse, distributed infrastructure and support detailed offline analysis of malware components.

With the above in mind, most financial institutions, credit unions and banks are working with legacy systems. While the hackers are working with advanced, flexible technology that allows them to be mobile, undetected and continue to be threat to your organization.

New threats are increasing in volumes and sophistication. These threats are designed to go undetected for long periods of time and require a technology capable of detecting attacks that traditional cyber defenses miss. They require a new far-seeing cybersecurity strategy and the right tools to support this strategy

  • Permissions, privileges and rights.
  • Intuitive analytics and behavioral pattern recognition to measure threat analysis.
  • Real-time advanced & intelligent cyber protection across your entire infrastructure both internally and externally.
  • Five security solutions under one application. No more working in silo’s and having to use multiple vendors.
  • Customized workflow with your existing security information event management system.
  • Customized tasks, responses, action, and event management.

Cybersecurity continues to remain a major challenge for financial institutions. By adopting an active cybersecurity strategy, a sound organizational structure, effective training and communications, and new technology. Financial institutions can begin to address their cyber security concerns and devote more resources to growth-oriented activities over time.
